Heavy metal © Wikipedia

Toxic metal
Inedible material
A toxic heavy metal is any relatively dense metal or metalloid that is noted for its potential toxicity, especially in environmental contexts. The term has particular application to cadmium, mercury, lead and arsenic, all of which appear in the World Health Organisation's list of 10 chemicals of major public concern.
Toxic heavy metals are found naturally in the earth, and become concentrated as a result of human caused activities. They enter plant, animal and human tissues via inhalation, diet and manual handling, and can bind to, and interfere with the functioning of vital cellular components. The toxic effects of arsenic, mercury and lead were known to the ancients but methodical studies of the toxicity of some heavy metals appear to date from only 1868. In humans, heavy metal poisoning is generally treated by the administration of chelating agents. Some elements regarded as heavy metals are essential, in small quantities, for human health.

Fipronil © Wikipedia

Pesticide
Insecticide
Fipronil is a broad-use insecticide that belongs to the phenylpyrazole chemical family. Fipronil is a broad-spectrum insecticide that disrupts the insect central nervous system by blocking GABA-gated chloride channels and glutamate-gated chloride (GluCl) channels, resulting in central nervous system toxicity. This causes hyperexcitation of contaminated insects' nerves and muscles. Specificity of fipronil on insects may come from a better efficacy on GABA receptor, but also because GluCl channels do not exist in mammals.

Designer's Message

There are a huge record of food safety scandal in Taiwan recently. It happens nearly every month. However, even though most Taiwanese people really care about eating healthy, it is still hardly seen a drameticly decrease on the scandal presented month by month. It hardly seen people in Taiwan do not really understand and take care on what food they have around.
This project is to influence people living in Taiwan to emphasize the danger of contaminated food through the past lessons. The project is to deliever an interactive multimedia solution. It will be presented as an interactive website for Taiwanese people to explore and experience the contaminated food scandals happening recently in Taiwan.
Sheng-Che Lin (Jerry)
Master of Multimedia Design
Monash University
